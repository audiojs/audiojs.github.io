# VST/plugin landscape × audiojs coverage

Researched 2026-07-10 — four parallel web sweeps (mix/master processors · creative FX · instruments · vocal/restoration/AI), grounded in vendor pages, Bedroom Producers Blog, KVR, MusicRadar/MusicTech/SoS, production-expert, vi-control, Plugin Boutique bestsellers, r/audioengineering / r/edmproduction. Companion to research.md § Landscape. Purpose: website positioning ("you know plugin X → here is the open atom") + honest gap map.

Legend: ✔ shipped+tested atom(s) · ◐ substrate (pieces shipped, product-shape/orchestrator missing) · ◌ stub/planned (neural lane etc.) · ✗ gap. Prices = typical street/list USD mid-2026.

## Punchlines (what the research actually proves)

1. **Restoration is the crown coverage.** RX 12 = $99/$399/$1,399 tiers. Practitioner consensus (vi-control "RX alternatives" thread, production-expert 2026): **spectral repair, dialogue isolate, music rebalance, hard-case de-reverb have no free counterpart** — free tier stops at Audacity noise reduction + RNNoise. `@audio/denoise` ships 15 atoms mirroring the RX module list (de-noise/hum/click/crackle/clip/wind/plosive/breath/reverb/ess + detect/gate/repair), incl. RX-class `denoise-repair` and `denoise-dereverb` — the only open *suite-shaped* answer. ML-tier modules (Dialogue Isolate, Rebalance) stay honestly ◌ `@audio/neural`.
2. **Feedback suppression: audiojs is the only open implementation in existence.** Researcher found none — KVR has years-old threads asking for one; the market is Waves X-FDBK $149, dbx AFS2 hardware $485, AlphaLabs De-Feedback $499 (AI, Nov 2024). `@audio/defeedback` (closed-electro-acoustic-loop verified) has zero open competitors.
3. **Micro-pitch widener: no confirmed OSS dual-voice micro-detune exists** (Soundtoys MicroShift ~$99 street is the reference). `@audio/spatial-microshift` (H3000 parity closed) fills a flagged-empty category.
4. **Spectral freeze/FX: flagged thin free tier** — SpecOps $149 / Crystallizer $149; the only OSS near-peer is PaulXStretch. audiojs ships `spectral-freeze` *and* `stretch-paulstretch` (plus sinusoidal SMS substrate).
5. **Dedicated delay: flagged gap** — "no feature-complete OSS tape-delay at EchoBoy/Valhalla level"; OSS answers are modules inside Surge XT. audiojs: `effect-{delay,multitap,pingpong,graindelay}` ✔ + tape-echo character composable (`saturate-tape` + `effect-lofi` wow/flutter).
6. **soothe2-class is hot and monetizable.** soothe2 $199 defined the category; **soothe3 $259 shipped 5/2026** ("the world's most popular mixing plugin gets a complete remake" — gearnews); Gullfoss $199, smart:EQ 4 $129; budget wave 2025-26 (DSEQ3 €79, KERN $29); first free entrants only in 2026 (ANINA 2/2026, Dagon 6/2026). `@audio/eq-dynamic` is built as exactly this class (band-energy-driven, hot band −3 dB+, transparent below threshold).
7. **Character compression set has no OSS equivalent.** LA-2A/1176/SSL-bus/Fairchild circuit emulations are UAD/Waves territory ($29–$99 each, endlessly re-sold); free tier = generic comps (Kotelnikov, DC1A). `dynamics-{opto,fet,vca,varimu}` are behaviorally verified circuit classes (T4 program-dependent release, FET attack speed, drive-dependent mu ratio) — as code, MIT.
8. **Time-stretch: the industry engine is closed B2B.** zplane Elastique licenses into Ableton/Cubase/FL/Studio One/Kontakt — not buyable as a lib; Serato Pitch 'n Time $799; OSS = Rubber Band (GPL, paid commercial license) + SoundTouch (LGPL, one algorithm). audiojs: **24 MIT atoms** (9 stretch + 15 shift: pvoc-lock, PGHI, SMS, PSOLA, WSOLA, granular, paulstretch, HPSS, formant-preserving…) — the broadest open algorithm set, permissively licensed where the OSS reference is copyleft.
9. **Open DSP winning categories is now proven, repeatedly.** NAM (MIT) grew ~300→25,000 community members in 2 years, its A2 architecture reportedly beats Neural DSP/ToneX in blind tests, and hardware vendors (Blackstar, HeadRush, NUX…) adopted the free format natively. Airwindows (MIT, 400+ plugins), Vital (GPL core), Surge XT (GPL, "industry standard among free tools"), Dexed, VCV/Cardinal — every category they entered, the price floor moved. Fresh micro-trend (Air-G Audio, 2026-07-07): indies productize OSS DSP with better GUIs. audiojs runs the same play at the **npm/library layer, which is empty** — essentia.js: no release in 5 years; Meyda: dead 2 years, still 14.6k dl/wk.
10. **Industry churn is a positioning gift.** NI preliminary insolvency (Jan 2026) → sold to inMusic (May 2026); iZotope exited NI → Boris FX (Jul 2026); Play.ht acquired by Meta and shut down (Dec 2025); Antares went subscription-first (Auto-Tune Unlimited $249/yr, lower perpetual SKUs retired); Waves' 2023 subscription revolt still remembered. The pitch writes itself factually: plugins are licenses to companies that get acquired; algorithms as MIT npm packages don't churn. Plus capabilities no plugin has: deterministic re-runs, CI/testability, headless/agent-native, browser-native, visible parameters.

## Honest gaps → roadmap candidates (ranked by leverage)

New atoms follow the **audio.js contract** — `~/projects/@audio/compile/CONTRACT.md` is the live source of truth (factory + params-convention manifest at `<pkg>/audio`, `"audio"` package.json field; GUIDE.md § Verified for worked examples). Length-preserving processors only (equal frames in/out — stretch-class stays batch API); `streaming: false` for whole-render kernels; generators read frames from outputs.
