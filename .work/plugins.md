## Punchlines (what the research actually proves)

1. **Restoration is the crown coverage.** RX 12 = $99/$399/$1,399 tiers. Practitioner consensus (vi-control "RX alternatives" thread, production-expert 2026): **spectral repair, dialogue isolate, music rebalance, hard-case de-reverb have no free counterpart** ‚Äî free tier stops at Audacity noise reduction + RNNoise. `@audio/denoise` ships 15 atoms mirroring the RX module list (de-noise/hum/click/crackle/clip/wind/plosive/breath/reverb/ess + detect/gate/repair), incl. RX-class `denoise-repair` and `denoise-dereverb` ‚Äî the only open *suite-shaped* answer. ML-tier modules (Dialogue Isolate, Rebalance) stay honestly ‚óå `@audio/neural`.
2. **Feedback suppression: audiojs is the only open implementation in existence.** Researcher found none ‚Äî KVR has years-old threads asking for one; the market is Waves X-FDBK $149, dbx AFS2 hardware $485, AlphaLabs De-Feedback $499 (AI, Nov 2024). `@audio/defeedback` (closed-electro-acoustic-loop verified) has zero open competitors.
3. **Micro-pitch widener: no confirmed OSS dual-voice micro-detune exists** (Soundtoys MicroShift ~$99 street is the reference). `@audio/spatial-microshift` (H3000 parity closed) fills a flagged-empty category.
4. **Spectral freeze/FX: flagged thin free tier** ‚Äî SpecOps $149 / Crystallizer $149; the only OSS near-peer is PaulXStretch. audiojs ships `spectral-freeze` *and* `stretch-paulstretch` (plus sinusoidal SMS substrate).
5. **Dedicated delay: flagged gap** ‚Äî "no feature-complete OSS tape-delay at EchoBoy/Valhalla level"; OSS answers are modules inside Surge XT. audiojs: `effect-{delay,multitap,pingpong,graindelay}` ‚úî + tape-echo character composable (`saturate-tape` + `effect-lofi` wow/flutter).
6. **soothe2-class is hot and monetizable.** soothe2 $199 defined the category; **soothe3 $259 shipped 5/2026** ("the world's most popular mixing plugin gets a complete remake" ‚Äî gearnews); Gullfoss $199, smart:EQ 4 $129; budget wave 2025-26 (DSEQ3 ‚Ç¨79, KERN $29); first free entrants only in 2026 (ANINA 2/2026, Dagon 6/2026). `@audio/eq-dynamic` is built as exactly this class (band-energy-driven, hot band ‚àí3 dB+, transparent below threshold).
7. **Character compression set has no OSS equivalent.** LA-2A/1176/SSL-bus/Fairchild circuit emulations are UAD/Waves territory ($29‚Äì$99 each, endlessly re-sold); free tier = generic comps (Kotelnikov, DC1A). `dynamics-{opto,fet,vca,varimu}` are behaviorally verified circuit classes (T4 program-dependent release, FET attack speed, drive-dependent mu ratio) ‚Äî as code, MIT.
8. **Time-stretch: the industry engine is closed B2B.** zplane Elastique licenses into Ableton/Cubase/FL/Studio One/Kontakt ‚Äî not buyable as a lib; Serato Pitch 'n Time $799; OSS = Rubber Band (GPL, paid commercial license) + SoundTouch (LGPL, one algorithm). audiojs: **24 MIT atoms** (9 stretch + 15 shift: pvoc-lock, PGHI, SMS, PSOLA, WSOLA, granular, paulstretch, HPSS, formant-preserving‚Ä¶) ‚Äî the broadest open algorithm set, permissively licensed where the OSS reference is copyleft.
9. **Open DSP winning categories is now proven, repeatedly.** NAM (MIT) grew ~300‚Üí25,000 community members in 2 years, its A2 architecture reportedly beats Neural DSP/ToneX in blind tests, and hardware vendors (Blackstar, HeadRush, NUX‚Ä¶) adopted the free format natively. Airwindows (MIT, 400+ plugins), Vital (GPL core), Surge XT (GPL, "industry standard among free tools"), Dexed, VCV/Cardinal ‚Äî every category they entered, the price floor moved. Fresh micro-trend (Air-G Audio, 2026-07-07): indies productize OSS DSP with better GUIs. audiojs runs the same play at the **npm/library layer, which is empty** ‚Äî essentia.js: no release in 5 years; Meyda: dead 2 years, still 14.6k dl/wk.
10. **Industry churn is a positioning gift.** NI preliminary insolvency (Jan 2026) ‚Üí sold to inMusic (May 2026); iZotope exited NI ‚Üí Boris FX (Jul 2026); Play.ht acquired by Meta and shut down (Dec 2025); Antares went subscription-first (Auto-Tune Unlimited $249/yr, lower perpetual SKUs retired); Waves' 2023 subscription revolt still remembered. The pitch writes itself factually: plugins are licenses to companies that get acquired; algorithms as MIT npm packages don't churn. Plus capabilities no plugin has: deterministic re-runs, CI/testability, headless/agent-native, browser-native, visible parameters.
